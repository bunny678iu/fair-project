2020 In Review

When the clocks struck midnight to begin the New Year of 2020, everyone was hoping to have an even better year than 2019. We looked into the new year with hopeful eyes to make it the best year possible, but no one knew what was about to happen. For most, 2020 was a year of challenges and improvements. One thing is for certain, 2020 was a year none of want to relive: especially through all its uncertainty and sorrows.

January

February

March

2020 did not exactly start off on the right foot, with the Australian bushfires and the impeachment hearings of President Donald Trump. Not only did the US get its first recorded COVID-19 case; but Kobe Bryant and his daughter, Gianna Bryant, died. These two, along with seven others, died in a tragic helicopter crash.

During the first weeks of the month, the first COVID-19 related death occurred in the state of California. President Trump was acquitted of all impeachment articles.

On the 13th, President Trump declared a national emergency to free up $50 billion to help US citizens. The Senate later approved a $2 trillion stimulus package for individuals and businesses. The CDC recommended that all gatherings of fifty or more people be canceled. Also, President Trump placed several travel bans on countries and closed the borders to help prevent the spread of COVID-19.


April

May

June

Not many influential things happened in April. COVID-19 continued to inflict fear around the globe, and candidates for the 2020 Presidential Election kept on with their campaigns, with several already backing out.

Throughout the beginning of the month, more COVID-19 related deaths occurred along with the continuous decline of our economy. New York City also shut down their subway system, something they had not done in the last 115 years, to help prevent the spread of COVID. On May 25th, George Floyd was killed during his arrest by Minneapolis Police. Floyd was the second race-related death that would spark country-wide protests. In May, the United States also passed a total of 100,000 COVID-19 deaths.

Several Black Lives Matter protests took place throughout the country in response to several uncalled deaths of innocent black people. Some of the protests were peaceful; but some did, unfortunately, get out of hand, creating dangerous situations.


July

August

September

In late July, Major League Baseball started a shortened season of just four months along with the NBA resuming their 2019-2020 season of play. On July 17th, John Lewis passed away after his six-month battle with cancer. John Lewis was a US Representative from the state of Georgia and was an influential member of the Civil Rights Movement.

The month of August started the long battle with the California Wildfire Complexes. These complexes went on to destroy hundreds of thousands of acres of land, ruined thousands of structures, and caused a few deaths. On August 23rd, Jacob Blake was shot multiple times by police officers in Kenosha, Wisconsin.

In response to the shooting in Kenosha, both 2020 Presidential candidates, Donald Trump and Joe Biden, visited the city to support the police. On September 18th, Supreme Court Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg died due to complications from cancer.


October

November

December

President Trump and the first lady both tested positive for COVID-19. In October, the first COVID-19 drug to treat the virus was also approved by the US FDA.

Days after election day, Joe Biden was predicted to be the new President; but Donald Trump refused to concede, asking for a recount. The first vaccines were sent in to get regulatory clearance for use in the United States.

The CDC recommended that health care providers be a part of the first line of people to receive the vaccines, which was approved for emergency used by the FDA. Late in the month, President Trump signed the $2.3 trillion coronavirus relief, hoping to avert another government shutdown.

As we turned the page from 2020 to 2021, we all hoped that the world would return to some type of normalcy. What will become of this new "normal" no one will truly know yet, but it is for the best that we try to find it in this new way of living. 2020 was filled with the losses of loved ones, stresses the virus created, and the distance between each other. With COVID came the canceling of all major events, sports seasons, and the start of a long era of online schooling. Our lives will be forever impacted by the events that occurred in the calendar year of 2020.

2020 was the year of masks and social distancing between oneself and others. A year of changes and quarantines, of loss and Zooms. An exhausting and chaotic year full of surreal and heartbreaking events. Broken dreams and missed opportunities. A year that showed perseverance and transformation by oneself and others. Front line workers whose dedication and determination are greatly appreciated for their work during this pandemic.
